# Voice System

## 1. Intent
This voice guide keeps language aligned with the design system: precise, calm, editorial, and useful.

## 2. Voice traits
- Clear before clever
- Specific before broad
- Evidence before rhetoric
- Calm before excited
- Structured before ornamental

## 3. Tone defaults

### Default tone
- Factual
- Compressed
- High signal
- Mildly authoritative

### Should sound like
- A sharp analyst
- A careful editor
- A credible operator

### Should not sound like
- A hype marketer
- A trend-chasing startup landing page
- A motivational speech
- A vague AI summary

## 4. Title rules
- Lead with the conclusion or topic, not a teaser.
- Prefer 6-18 Chinese characters for major headings when possible.
- Avoid stacked metaphors and slogan-style language.
- Use parallel structure across sibling headings.

Good patterns:
- `增长放缓，但留存稳定`
- `三类风险正在集中暴露`
- `渠道效率的拐点已经出现`

Avoid:
- `重新定义未来增长`
- `开启下一代智能革命`
- `用数据驱动无限可能`

## 5. Summary rules
- The first paragraph or summary strip should answer: what happened, why it matters, what to look at next.
- Keep summaries short and directional.
- If there is data, include the anchor number early.

## 6. Paragraph rules
- One paragraph, one idea.
- Prefer short to medium sentences.
- Put the key clause early.
- Use lists only when comparison or enumeration improves scanning.

## 7. Evidence rules
- Claims should be paired with data, examples, or explicit uncertainty.
- If evidence is partial, say so plainly.
- When data is estimated, use qualified language instead of false precision.

## 8. CTA and conclusion rules
- End with next step, implication, or decision.
- Avoid inflated emotional closers.
- In decks and reports, the final line should feel operational.

## 9. Formatting conventions
- Numbers use consistent unit style within one artifact.
- English terms are used only when more precise than Chinese.
- Abbreviations should be expanded once in articles and reports.

## 10. Prohibited language
- Empty intensifiers: `颠覆`, `革命性`, `极致`, `空前`
- Generic AI filler: `随着...的不断发展`, `在当今快速变化的环境中`
- Overwritten transitions: `值得一提的是`, `不难发现`, `毋庸置疑`

## 11. Artifact-specific voice

### Web page
- Slightly more inviting
- Still concise
- Can use shorter blocks and interface labels

### PPT
- Hard conclusion per slide
- Fewer subordinate clauses
- More punch in titles, less prose in body

### Infographic
- Very compressed
- Strong labels and micro-explanations

### Article
- Most room for rhythm and narrative
- Must still stay informational, not literary

### Stats report
- Most literal and method-aware
- Avoid interpretive overreach
